Phishing Awareness

close

Phishing is when someone tries to trick you into sharing personal information like passwords, bank details, or OTPs by pretending to be a trusted person or company.

How Phishing Happens

  • Fake emails or SMS saying "Your bank account is blocked"
  • Calls pretending to be from a bank, delivery service, or government
  • WhatsApp messages asking you to click unknown links
  • Fake lottery or gift messages

How to Spot a Phishing Attempt

  • Unknown or strange phone numbers
  • Messages with spelling mistakes
  • Pressure words like "urgent", "immediately", "your account will close"
  • Links that look odd or unfamiliar
  • Asking for OTP or password

What You Should Do

  • Never share OTPs or passwords
  • Do not click unknown links
  • Call the real company using their official number
  • Delete the message or block the sender

Spam Callers

close

Why Block Spam Calls?

Spam callers waste time and may try to cheat you.

On Android Phones

  • Open Phone app
  • Go to Recent Calls
  • Tap number
  • Select Block / Report Spam

On iPhones

  • Open Phone app
  • Go to Recents
  • Tap (i) next to number
  • Choose Block this Caller

Extra Safety Tips

  • Do not answer calls from unknown international numbers
  • If someone asks for bank details, hang up immediately
  • Use apps like Truecaller (optional) to identify spam calls

WhatsApp Practices

close

What Not to Share

  • OTPs
  • Bank photos or documents
  • Personal details like address, PAN, Aadhaar
  • Forwarding unknown messages

How to Stay Safe

  • Enable two-step verification
    WhatsApp → Settings → Account → Two-step Verification → Set a 6-digit PIN
  • Only accept messages from known contacts
  • Do not click blue-colored unknown links
  • Do not join random WhatsApp groups
  • Be careful with video calls from strangers

Signs of a Scam on WhatsApp

  • Someone pretending to be a relative asking for money
  • Messages with "urgent help needed"
  • Links offering gifts, coupons, lottery

Password Management

close

What Is a Password Manager?

A password manager is an app that remembers your passwords for you, so you only need to remember one master password.

Easy Steps (General Guide)

  1. Install a Password Manager
    • Bitwarden (free)
    • 1Password
    • Google Password Manager (built-in)
  2. Create an Account
    • Use your email
    • Set a strong but memorable master password
      Example: BlueTreeHouse92!
  3. Save a New Password
    • When logging into any website
    • The app will ask: "Save password?"
    • Press Yes / Save
  4. Auto-Fill Passwords
    • Next time you open a website, it will fill password for you
    • You don't need to type anything

Safety Tips

  • Never share your master password
  • Use passwords with numbers + letters + symbols
  • Do not write passwords on paper in public places
  • Enable 2-Factor Authentication when possible
